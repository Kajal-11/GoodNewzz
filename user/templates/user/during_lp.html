During Pregnancy

DO'S

Things to keep in mind during pregnancy.

An early consultation with the obstetrician and regular follow-up is essential during the first trimester for a weekly progress on the development of the foetus.

Choose the obstetrician and hospital with care. Avoid frequent changes as this leads to conflicting opinions during consultations.

Increase the intake of folic acid supplements as it’s a type of vitamin B that is needed for the formation of blood cells.

Do consume more of organic and homemade food, since this is rich in minerals and essential vitamins and proteins. Eat a variety of healthy food that is rich in iron, calcium and folate.

Small frequent meals at regular intervals, along with an adequate liquid intake, are essential during the first trimester.

Do choose foods with antioxidants throughout pregnancy such as dark green spinach, oranges, carrots, red apples, yellow bananas, blueberries.

Drink at least eight glasses (1.5 litres) of fluid every day, mostly water. It’s very important to stay hydrated.

Walking plays a critical role and is considered to be the best exercise for pregnant women. It helps to keep your muscles toned and, most importantly, keeps you active.

DONT'S

Things to avoid during pregnancy.

Smoking, drinking alcohol or drugs may be detrimental to your baby’s growth, development and health.

Do not take any self-medication as there could be a health-risk for the mother and child.

Do not consume any junk foods as they lead to an increased risk of gestational diabetes.

Do not consume raw papaya and raw pineapple. Avoid foods with preservatives and pesticides.

Avoid dehydration, stress, over exertion and aggressive exercises.

Prevent weight gain till 16 weeks of pregnancy.

Don’t get into the sauna or steam room or overheat in the bath.

Do not consume street foods to avoid gastrointestinal problems.
