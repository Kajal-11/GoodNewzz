After Pregnancy

DO'S

Things to keep in mind after pregnancy.

Starting a birth control method immediately after you have a baby can help you avoid an unintended pregnancy. It also lets you control if or when you want to become pregnant again.

Choose the obstetrician and hospital with care. Avoid frequent changes as this leads to conflicting opinions during consultations.

Do consume more of organic and homemade food, since this is rich in minerals and essential vitamins and proteins.

If you think you may have postpartum depression, or if your partner or family members are concerned that you do, it is important to see your ob-gyn or other health care professional as soon as possible.

Pelvic floor exercises, also called Kegel exercises, are used to strengthen the muscles that surround the openings of the urethra, vagina, and rectum.

Do choose foods with antioxidants throughout pregnancy such as dark green spinach, oranges, carrots, red apples, yellow bananas, blueberries.

Drink at least eight glasses (1.5 litres) of fluid every day, mostly water. It’s very important to stay hydrated. Drink fluids as much as possible.

If you are diagnosed with heart disease during pregnancy, you may need to see your ob-gyn earlier or more often after childbirth so they can keep a close eye on your health.

DONT'S

Things to avoid after pregnancy.

. Don’t strain when trying to poop. The muscles you use for pooping are the same muscles that you overexerted during childbirth.

Smoking, drinking alcohol or drugs may be detrimental to development and health.

Don’t use tampons until after you’ve seen your doctor for your post-natal check.

Don’t wear clothes that are tight around your perineal area. Not only will these be uncomfortable, it also increases the risk of infection.

Avoid dehydration, stress, over exertion and aggressive exercises.

Don’t engage in high impact exercises and activities.

Don’t get into the sauna or steam room or overheat in the bath.

Do not consume street foods to avoid gastrointestinal problems.
